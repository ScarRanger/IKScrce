Indian and Gregorian Calendar

Indian Calendar: The Hindu calendar, also known as the Panchang, is based on the lunisolar system, incorporating both solar and lunar cycles. It includes months like Chaitra, Vaisakha, and festivals aligned with astronomical events like solstices and equinoxes.

Gregorian Calendar: Introduced by Pope Gregory XIII in 1582, this solar calendar is used worldwide today. It refined the Julian calendar by adjusting leap years to align more closely with Earthâ€™s orbit around the Sun.

Comparison: The Indian calendar considers lunar cycles and planetary movements for religious and agricultural purposes, while the Gregorian calendar is purely solar-based and used for civil purposes.
